A popular trend over the past year or two is using landscapes, cityscapes and seascapes in a design. Often placed in a website's footer, they're a great way to show off your creativity and as a web designer gives you a chance to test out your Illustrator skills.

<!--more-->
<h4>A few tips for designing your 'scape'</h4>
<h5>1. Get some stock photography</h5>
You can get lots of downloadable vector graphics and photos from <a href="http://www.istockphoto.com/file_search.php?text=landscape&amp;action=file">iStockphoto</a>.
<h5>2. Check out Flickr</h5>
Flickr has loads of <a href="http://flickr.com/search/?q=landscape&amp;l=cc&amp;ss=0&amp;ct=0&amp;w=all">Creative Commons photos</a> available for use.
<h5>3. Take your own photos</h5>
Get out around your town/city and take a few <a href="http://flickr.com/photos/leemunroe/2428194839/">photos of noticeable buildings</a>.
<h5>4. Sketch your own ideas</h5>
Jot down your own hills and buildings on paper.
<h5>5. Finally, get tracing and cutting in Illustrator and Photoshop</h5>
Now you have your inspiration you can start tracing in illustrator and trying different Photoshop effects on your buildings to come up with the perfect amazing scape.
