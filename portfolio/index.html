Web & mobile design portfolio of Lee Munroe

Companies & startups I've worked with

Mailgun by Rackspace

The email service for developers.

I joined Mailgun in 2013 to lead UX. I was their first design hire and joined their small team of talented back-end engineers. Mailgun is an email API service for developers. After having gone through Y-Combinator it was successfully acquired by Rackspace in 2012.

My role involves product design and front-end development, working closely with our team of engineers, operations and marketing.

Visit Mailgun

Flask.io

Create really simple and sharable to do lists with Flask. No sign up. Nothing to download. Just add tasks.

Flask is a side project that I designed and developed myself using Ruby on Rails. It started off as a weekend experiment to learn CoffeeScript. I now use it as a playground to experiment with new ideas, tools and keep my development skills up to date.

Visit Flask

CodeShare.io

Share code real time with other developers.

In 2013 I hacked together this free code sharing tool with Firebase, using Node.js and Express. The purpose of this project was to learn Node.js and experiment with the Firebase API.

Copy/paste and share real-time code snippets with others. If you share the url with a friend they will see your code as you type it.

Visit CodeShare

Runnable

In 2012 I joined Runnable, becoming a member of the founding team and sole Designer. Runnable enables developers to run server-side code in the browser. No downloading, nothing to install, no registration, just run code. They have since launched and went on to raise a $2m seed round, led by Sierra Ventures.

Daily activities involved product discovery, prototyping, user studies, interface design, HTML/CSS markup, integration with Node.js and Javascript and working closely with the engineers and founder.

As an early stage lean startup, I spent a lot of time running experiments, talking to users and using tools like Mixpanel, Optimizely and Qualaroo to help validate assumptions and determine the product development roadmap.

Visit Runnable

Kareo

Based in Irvine, Orange County CA, Kareo develop EHR and Practice Management software.

I joined as their first Design hire, then led the UX Team from 2011-2012 helping them transition from a desktop to web application, and introducing their first mobile app.

Daily activities involved managing the design team, product discovery, talking to customers, lots of prototyping, running usability tests, designing and marking up the UI and working closely with developers.

Visit Kareo

Twit Amore

Twit Amore was a project of mine that went live on Valentine's Day 2012.

It's a quirky website that tells you who you love on Twitter. I used it as an experiment to learn some Ruby on Rails, along with Haml, Sass, Twitter API and was my first project using Heroku.

Due to the tie in with Valentine's Day the site went viral and managed to get on the front page of Mashable, The Next Web and Huffington Post among others. The site received over 1 million unique visitors in 2012.

Visit Twit Amore

Lookaly

The best way to describe Lookaly is Yelp for Northern Ireland.

As Creative Director and Co-Founder I designed the user experience, interface, branding, HTML/CSS and took care of product, marketing and sales.

The site is well received in Northern Ireland attracting over 40 thousand unique visitors per month.

Visit Lookaly

The Big Word Project

Back in University I teamed up with classmate Paddy Donnelly and created a website that let people buy words of the dictionary, priced at $1 per letter, and link them to their website.

It was a viral success. We managed to sell over 7,500 words and it led to a feature article in Wired magazine.

Visit TBWP
